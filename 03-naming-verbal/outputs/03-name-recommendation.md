## Important framing

This document presents a recommendation and a runner-up with honest tradeoffs. The final decision is a stakeholder decision, not an agent decision. The recommendation is the strongest candidate given the product's positioning, tone, and availability picture. It is not the only viable name.

---

## Recommendation: Pavilion

### Rationale

The product is designed to feel like the institutional heart of amateur cricket — calm, reliable, literate, trusted. The pavilion is exactly that, in physical form, at every cricket ground in the country. It is where fixtures are discussed, where the fixture secretary operates, where the scorecards are posted, where tea is made between innings. It is the centre of the club's organisational life, not its playing life.

This is the right register. Not a batting or bowling term (those are the clichés). Not a Silicon Valley portmanteau. Not a generic sports-coordination word. A place — specific to cricket, immediately legible to the audience, warm without being nostalgic.

**Against the product principles (from Agent 1):**
- *Calm, institutional-in-a-good-way* — Pavilion scores highest on this axis
- *National Trust / Ordnance Survey comparison* — Pavilion fits this register; FixtureDesk does not
- *Cricket-literate without being nostalgic* — Pavilion is architectural, not romanticised; it refers to a building and a function, not a golden era
- *Never "flashy"* — one word; no cleverness required

**Domain strategy:** `pavilioncc.co.uk` as primary (cc = cricket club, standard abbreviation known to all target users). Secondary: `pavilion.cricket` if available. Agent 10 / procurement to action once approved.

### Risks of this recommendation

1. **Domain requires suffix.** `pavilion.co.uk` and `pavilion.com` are taken. This is manageable but requires committing to `pavilioncc.co.uk` or equivalent. The name's strength compensates for the suffix cost.

2. **Does not telegraph function.** Cold users cannot infer "fixture coordination" from "Pavilion" alone. This requires brand to do more work — specifically the tagline and app store description. This is a brand (Agent 5) and content (Agent 8) dependency.

3. **Visual identity risk.** If Agent 5 takes Pavilion's architecture reference in a heritage/sepia direction, it will undermine the non-nostalgic positioning. This is a constraint to communicate explicitly in handoff: the name references function (the building as organisational hub), not aesthetics (Victorian pavilion, red-ball romance).

4. **Trademark is a common English word.** Requires proper legal review before filing. A standalone common noun is harder to register as a trademark, though the specific class usage mitigates this.

### Pronunciation note

No ambiguity. "Pav-ILL-yun." Three syllables. UK English: no regional variation that would cause confusion.

### Logo considerations

- One word, eight letters, good proportion — works in horizontal lockup
- Strong letterforms: the "P" and double "l" give the monogram options (P alone is clean; a "P" monogram is common but readable)
- The word suggests a roofline silhouette without requiring literal architectural illustration — mark can abstract this
- Works in single colour; scales to icon size
- No awkward capitalisation or camel-case required

## Runner-up: FixtureDesk

### Rationale

FixtureDesk is the strongest plain-functional candidate. It uses the product's canonical term ("fixture") in the name, it telegraphs purpose immediately, and it has the best domain availability of any shortlisted name. If the primary recommendation is rejected or its domain situation becomes unworkable, FixtureDesk is a viable and defensible alternative.

The trade is clear: FixtureDesk gives up warmth and register for legibility and domain security. The product can succeed with either name; the positioning and voice work equally well around both.

### What would cause a switch to the runner-up

1. `pavilioncc.co.uk` and all realistic Pavilion suffix variants are taken
2. Trademark concerns surface that require abandoning the name after stakeholder approval (avoidable with early legal check)
3. Stakeholder preference for a name that more explicitly communicates product function — a legitimate choice if the primary audience skews toward pragmatic fixture managers over cricket-cultural familiarity
4. Agent 5 (Brand Identity) cannot execute a non-nostalgic visual identity on "Pavilion" — if every execution looks like a heritage cricket tin, the name becomes a liability

### Runner-up risks

- "Desk" naming convention is associated with 2010s SaaS products (Zendesk, FreshDesk) — may not age as well
- Less distinctive in a market scan; harder to build emotional resonance
- Does not benefit from the comparison points (National Trust, Ordnance Survey) the way Pavilion does

## Handoff notes for Agent 5 (Brand Identity)

If Pavilion is approved:
- The reference is functional (the hub of club administration), not aesthetic (heritage/nostalgic)
- Avoid Victorian architecture illustration; the mark should abstract the pavilion concept
- The tone is: a well-run county club's modern website. Not a classic cricket annual.
- The `.cricket` or `.co.uk` domain signals UK focus — lean into this, not against it

If FixtureDesk is approved:
- The name does functional work; the brand must add personality
- Monogram FD is clean but generic — invest in a distinctive wordmark instead
- The "Desk" framing supports a calm, service-desk visual vocabulary — restraint is appropriate
