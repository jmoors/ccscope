# Agent 4 — Discovery & Research

## Briefing

### Purpose
Reality-check every assumption with actual users. Recommend a seed geography. Develop launch partnerships. Feed findings back into scope, domain model, and IA.

### Why it matters
A search-first marketplace has a brutal cold-start problem. Assumptions about how clubs arrange fixtures, what they'd trust, and whether they'd switch from existing tools must be tested, not assumed. Wrong assumptions here kill the product.

### Deliverables
1. Interview plan and guide
2. 15–20 fixture manager interviews (synthesised)
3. 3–5 league administrator interviews
4. 3–5 umpire / umpire association interviews
5. Current-workflow documentation (how clubs arrange friendlies today)
6. Assumption validation report (which assumptions held, which broke)
7. Seed geography recommendation with rationale
8. Launch partnership leads (1–2 developed to warm introduction)
9. Ongoing research cadence recommendation

### Inputs you provide
- Agent 1 outputs (scope, positioning)
- Agent 2 outputs (domain model, algorithm proposals)
- UK cricket ecosystem context (leagues, counties, ECB)
- Interviewee recruitment channels

### Expected outputs
- `01-interview-plan.md`
- `02-fixture-manager-interviews-synthesis.md`
- `03-league-admin-interviews-synthesis.md`
- `04-umpire-interviews-synthesis.md`
- `05-current-workflow.md`
- `06-assumption-validation.md`
- `07-seed-geography-recommendation.md`
- `08-partnership-leads.md`
- `09-ongoing-research-plan.md`

### Timing
Weeks 1–8 (starts immediately, runs long). Early findings feed Agents 2 and 6 by week 4.

### Handoff criteria
- Minimum 15 fixture manager interviews completed and synthesised
- Seed geography decision made with stakeholder alignment
- At least one partnership conversation at "warm lead" stage
- Clear flag of any original assumption that research invalidated

### Common failure modes
- Leading questions that confirm existing assumptions
- Over-sampling enthusiastic early adopters (survivorship bias)
- Not interviewing anyone who currently uses Play-Cricket happily
- Treating research as one-off rather than ongoing
- Failing to separate "what users say they want" from "what they actually do"
