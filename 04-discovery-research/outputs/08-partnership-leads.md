## Purpose and scope

This document tracks every partnership lead from identification through to warm introduction. Agent 4's responsibility ends when a lead is at warm stage — a named contact who has agreed to a follow-up conversation. Agent 10 then owns the relationship from that point forward.

"Partnership" here means any external organisation whose cooperation would meaningfully accelerate platform adoption or research access. It does not mean commercial partnership, sponsorship, or financial arrangement unless explicitly flagged.

**Decision rights note:** Per `shared/05-decision-rights.md`, partnership decisions are owned by Agent 10 with Agent 1 consulted. Agent 4 develops leads and hands off — we do not commit on behalf of the project.

## Lead 1 — Surrey Cricket Board (SCB)

**Status:** Identified
**Priority:** High
**Type:** County governing body (recreational cricket)
**Geography:** Surrey

### What they are

Surrey Cricket Board is the county-level body that supports recreational and developmental cricket across Surrey, independent of Surrey County Cricket Club. SCB employs club development officers and coordinates with ECB on recreational cricket programmes. They have existing relationships with most affiliated clubs in the county and manage some Play-Cricket administration at the county level.

### Why this matters

SCB is the most direct route to fixture managers across Surrey. An SCB introduction transforms cold outreach to clubs into a warm referral. For the research phase, this means better interview recruitment and less selection bias. For launch, SCB-distributed communications to clubs have significantly higher credibility than direct marketing from an unknown platform.

### What we are asking for (staged)

**Stage 1 — Research phase (now):**
- Introductions to 6–10 fixture managers across different clubs, formats, and areas of Surrey for research conversations
- Permission to mention in recruitment that we were introduced by SCB

**Stage 2 — Launch phase (Agent 10):**
- Willingness to share information about the platform with clubs they support (via newsletter, club development officer conversations, or similar)
- This is not an endorsement request; it is an information-sharing request

**We are not asking for:**
- Financial contribution
- Formal endorsement or co-branding
- Exclusive territory
- Any commitment before the product exists

### Contact route

ECB recreational cricket → Surrey Cricket Board Development Manager. Alternative: direct approach to SCB via their website contact form, referencing recreational club support programme.

The club development officer role (held by one or more staff members) is the correct entry point. The Board itself is the governance layer; the operational team is who we need.

### Warm lead criterion

A named SCB staff member (development officer or equivalent) who has agreed to a 20-minute call to hear about the project and discuss whether introductions to clubs are possible.

### Risks

- SCB may have concerns about a new platform competing with or fragmenting Play-Cricket administration. This must be addressed directly: we are a friendly fixture coordination tool, not a league management or administrative system.
- SCB staff capacity is variable — cricket administration is often volunteer or part-time at county level. Response times may be slow.
- SCB may be neutral or sceptical. This is acceptable; we need the introductions, not the endorsement.

### Next actions

1. Identify named SCB development officer via ECB recreational cricket network or SCB website.
2. Draft brief introductory email (2 paragraphs: who we are, what we want, why it might interest them).
3. Send. Follow up once at 10 days if no response.

## Lead 2 — A Surrey League Secretary

**Status:** Identified
**Priority:** Medium
**Type:** Amateur cricket league administrator
**Geography:** Surrey

### What they are

Surrey has several recreational cricket leagues, including the Surrey Championship (the senior amateur league) and multiple divisions below it, as well as format-specific and area leagues. League secretaries sit at the centre of fixture coordination for league games and often have informal influence over how clubs arrange their friendlies.

### Why this matters

A league secretary knows which clubs in their league struggle to fill friendly fixture lists. They can identify the most useful research participants and — for launch — act as a credible voice recommending the platform to clubs they work with.

### What we are asking for

**Stage 1 — Research:**
- Names of fixture managers at 3–5 clubs who actively arrange friendlies and would be willing to talk to us
- Ideally including at least one club that has had reliability problems with opponents

**Stage 2 — Launch (Agent 10):**
- A mention in the league's end-of-season or pre-season communication to clubs
- This is not a formal partnership; it is a single communication

### Which league to approach first

The Surrey County Cricket League (covering multiple divisions, broad reach across Surrey clubs) is the recommended first approach over the Surrey Championship. The Championship attracts the more competitive end of the amateur game; the SCCL has better coverage of the social and mid-table clubs who are our primary users.

This should be confirmed in initial research conversations — ask interviewees which league they're in and how they describe the league's culture.

### Warm lead criterion

A named league secretary who has agreed to pass our recruitment notice to clubs in their league, or who has provided direct introductions to 2+ fixture managers.

### Risks

- League secretaries are almost universally volunteers with significant existing workload during the season.
- Some leagues have quasi-exclusive relationships with existing tools (PlayCricket, SportyHQ). Understand before approaching.
- A league-level endorsement that reaches Play-Cricket-loyal clubs may trigger defensive reactions. Frame carefully.

### Next actions

1. Identify league secretaries via Play-Cricket's league directory (publicly accessible).
2. Prioritise leagues that manage 10+ clubs across multiple divisions (broader reach than single-division leagues).
3. Initial contact by email during the pre-season window (February–April) when secretaries are active but not overwhelmed.

**Note:** Given current date (2026-04-19), we are at the start of the season. Outreach should happen this week; mid-season contact will be harder to get a response to.

## Lead 3 — Association of Cricket Umpires and Scorers (ACUS), Surrey Branch

**Status:** Identified
**Priority:** Medium
**Type:** Umpire association (county branch)
**Geography:** Surrey (national body based in England)

### What they are

The Association of Cricket Umpires and Scorers (ACUS) is the governing body for cricket umpires and scorers in England and Wales. It operates through county branches, each of which maintains a register of umpires and supports their development. The Surrey branch coordinates umpires for recreational cricket across the county.

### Why this matters

**Research:** ACUS Surrey is the fastest route to recruiting umpire interview participants — 3 to 5 umpires for Guide C interviews. Without a structured route to umpires, cold recruitment is unreliable.

**Launch:** The platform's v1 umpire feature is scoped to club rosters only (not an open marketplace). However, ACUS's awareness of the platform matters for v2 planning, and their cooperation during research builds goodwill for future conversations.

### What we are asking for

**Stage 1 — Research:**
- Permission to include a brief recruitment notice in ACUS Surrey communications asking for umpires willing to participate in a 30-minute research conversation
- Or: direct introductions to 3–5 umpires with different levels of experience

**Stage 2 — Launch (Agent 10):**
- An informational conversation about how the platform's umpire roster feature works and whether it raises any concerns from their perspective
- This is about avoiding surprises, not about securing endorsement

### Warm lead criterion

Named ACUS Surrey branch contact who has agreed to pass on our recruitment notice, or who has directly provided 2+ umpire introductions.

### Risks

- ACUS may be protective of umpire data and relationships. Approach as information-sharing, not list-access.
- If ACUS perceives the platform as eventually building an open umpire marketplace (which it may, in v2), they may have concerns about disintermediation. Be honest: v1 is club-roster only. Do not make promises about v2.

### Next actions

1. Identify ACUS Surrey branch secretary via ACUS national website.
2. Draft brief note explaining the research purpose and requesting a short call or permission to share a recruitment notice.

## Lead 4 — ECB Recreational Cricket

**Status:** Identified
**Priority:** Low–Medium (high leverage if successful; low probability of quick response)
**Type:** National governing body
**Geography:** National

### What they are

ECB (England and Wales Cricket Board) runs recreational cricket development through a network of county boards and club development officers. The recreational cricket team at ECB focuses on increasing participation and supporting clubs. ECB also owns and operates Play-Cricket.

### Why this matters

An ECB-level relationship has two distinct values:
1. **Credibility signal:** If ECB is aware of the platform and neutral-to-positive, county boards are more likely to engage. We do not need ECB endorsement; we need them to not actively discourage county boards from engaging.
2. **Play-Cricket co-existence:** Early intelligence on whether ECB would view the platform as complementary or competitive to Play-Cricket is strategically important. A platform that ECB considers a threat will find county-board partnership much harder.

### What we are asking for

A 20-minute conversation with an ECB recreational cricket lead to:
- Describe what the platform does
- Understand whether ECB has concerns from a Play-Cricket co-existence perspective
- Understand whether there are county boards ECB would recommend as open to new recreational tools

### Warm lead criterion

Named ECB contact who has confirmed awareness of the project and agreed that county-board engagement is their decision (not requiring ECB clearance).

### Risks

- ECB is a large, slow-moving organisation. Response times can be weeks.
- ECB may view any new cricket platform with suspicion given their investment in Play-Cricket.
- Escalation risk: if ECB becomes negatively engaged (actively discouraging county boards), it creates a real problem. Approach carefully; position as complementary, not competitive.
- Low probability of a useful response in the research window. Do not depend on this lead for research recruitment.

### Next actions

1. Do not lead with ECB outreach. Establish Surrey Cricket Board and league secretary leads first.
2. Approach ECB recreational cricket only after Surrey Cricket Board has agreed to engage — an existing county board relationship demonstrates legitimacy.
3. Approach via named individual, not generic contact form.

## Handoff checklist (for Agent 10)

When a lead reaches warm status, the following are passed to Agent 10:

- [ ] Named contact: full name, role, organisation, email
- [ ] Summary of conversations to date (what was said, what was agreed)
- [ ] What we have offered or implied (to avoid contradictions)
- [ ] What we have explicitly ruled out (so Agent 10 doesn't accidentally re-offer)
- [ ] The contact's stated interests or concerns
- [ ] Recommended next step and framing

Agent 4 remains available for questions on research context but does not attend further partnership meetings unless specifically requested.
