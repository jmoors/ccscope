## One-sentence product definition

A calm, reliable platform that helps cricket clubs find games and fill gaps — while quietly rewarding good behaviour over time.

*(Source: `shared/PROJECT_CONTEXT.md`. Reproduced here as the canonical anchor for this document.)*

---

## Problem statement

Amateur cricket clubs across the UK waste hours every week trying to arrange friendlies: chasing contacts through WhatsApp threads, duplicate posts on Facebook groups, no-shows with no consequence, and fixtures falling apart because the second umpire bailed. The current state is informal, fragmented, and invisible to anyone outside a fixed network.

The core pain:
- Finding an opponent takes too long and relies on who you know.
- Gaps in fixtures (venue, officials, opposition) are hard to fill at short notice.
- Unreliable clubs create no friction for themselves but plenty for others.
- New clubs have no way to establish credibility.

We remove the friction and the rewiring of informal trust, without replacing the human relationships underneath.

---

## Target users

**Primary — Team / Fixture Manager**  
The person who sorts the games. Often a captain, secretary, or senior player. Manages 10–30 fixtures per season. Currently working across email, WhatsApp, and Play-Cricket. Time-poor; high frustration tolerance; wants reliability, not cleverness.

**Secondary — Club Administrator**  
Sets up the club profile, teams, and venues. May be the same person as the Fixture Manager in small clubs. Works on desktop, evenings. Needs clarity over features.

**Tertiary — Service Provider**  
Umpires, scorers, coaches (v1: umpires via club roster only). May use the platform infrequently and only in response to requests.

---

## Category positioning

**What we are:**  
A structured coordination tool for amateur cricket fixture management. The closest analogy is a specialist directory with built-in request flows and a quiet trust layer.

**What we are not:**
- A social network or community platform
- A booking/ticketing system
- A league management tool (we coexist with Play-Cricket; we don't replace it)
- A review platform or reputation site
- A marketplace in the commercial sense

---

## Must-embody adjectives

1. **Reliable** — does what it says; no surprises; consistent across sessions
2. **Calm** — no urgency theatre, no dark patterns, no gamification
3. **Literate** — cricket-aware without being nostalgic; speaks the language naturally
4. **Trusted** — inspires confidence in the system and in other users
5. **Direct** — minimal clicks, minimal copy, no hedging

---

## Must-avoid adjectives

1. **Flashy** — no gratuitous animation, no app-store-optimised UI patterns
2. **Gamified** — no streaks, no leaderboards, no achievement unlocks
3. **Loud** — no sports-media energy, no exclamation marks in system messages
4. **Opaque** — trust mechanics must never feel like a black box
5. **Nostalgic** — not a heritage brand; not sepia-toned cricket romanticism

---

## Comparison points

**Feel like:**
- National Trust digital products (calm authority, clear purpose, non-commercial)
- Ordnance Survey Maps (functional, trusted, domain-specific, used by people who know what they need)
- A well-run county club's modern website (competent, literate, not trying too hard)
- GOV.UK (direct copy, no marketing language, respects the user's time)

**Do not feel like:**
- Strava (competitive, streak-driven, social feed)
- Eventbrite (commercial, transactional, generic)
- SportyHQ / PlayHQ (cluttered; feature-maximalist)
- Betway / Sky Bet (betting-adjacent colour palettes and urgency language)
- Facebook Groups (the problem we're solving, not the solution)
