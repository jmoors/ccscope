## Core voice principles

### 1. Say the thing directly

No preamble. No throat-clearing. No "We noticed that you might want to…". State the fact or the action.

### 2. Respect the user's cricket knowledge

They know what a timed declaration is. They know the difference between a 1st XI and a Sunday XI. Don't explain the sport. Do explain the platform.

### 3. Earn trust through specificity

Vague copy signals vague thinking. Specific copy signals a system that knows what it's doing. Dates, formats, team names: always specific.

### 4. Neutral on outcome, clear on action

We do not celebrate match confirmations with confetti. We do not shame late cancellations with red banners. We state what happened and what happens next.

### 5. Institutional without being cold

Think: the club secretary who has been doing this for 20 years. Competent, considered, not warm-fuzzy. Helpful because they know the answer, not because they're performing helpfulness.

---

## Tone variations by context

| Context | Tone | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Onboarding | Clear, orienting | One thing at a time. No "Welcome to [Product]!" opener. |
| Search / browse | Neutral, functional | Labels and filters only. No persuasive copy. |
| Empty state | Honest, practical | Explain why it's empty and what to do. No "Looks like..." |
| Success | Understated | Confirm the action. Skip the celebration. |
| Error | Direct, actionable | What happened. What to do. No apology theatre. |
| Notification | Specific, timely | Subject line = the fact. Body = what's needed. |
| Trust / review | Private, factual | No public shaming. No performance of community. |

## Do / Don't pairs

### Onboarding

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "Post your availability for the dates you need a game." | "Hey there! Let's get you set up and ready to play!" |
| "Your club profile is visible to other teams searching for opponents." | "Great news — you're almost ready to start connecting with the cricket community!" |

### Empty state

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "No availability found for those dates. Post your own availability to be found by other teams." | "Looks like it's quiet around here. Be the first to post!" |
| "No umpires in your club's roster yet. Ask your Club Administrator to add them." | "Oops! No umpires found. Try widening your search!" |

### Match request

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "Match request sent to [Team Name]. They have 7 days to respond." | "Your invite is on its way! Fingers crossed!" |
| "You have a match request from [Team Name] for [Date]. Accept or decline." | "You've got a new connection request! Don't leave them hanging." |

### Success

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "Fixture confirmed. Both teams have been notified." | "Amazing! Your fixture is locked in — have a great game!" |
| "Availability posted for [Date]." | "You're live! Teams can now find you." |

### Error

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "That date is already taken by another fixture. Choose a different date." | "Oops! Something went wrong. Please try again." |
| "You don't have permission to edit this team. Contact your Club Administrator." | "Uh-oh! It seems you can't do that right now." |

### Notification (email subject lines)

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "[Team Name] has sent you a match request for [Date]" | "You have a new message on [Product]!" |
| "Match request accepted — fixture on [Date] is confirmed" | "Great news from [Product]!" |

### Trust / review

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "How did the match go? Your review is private and helps other teams find reliable opponents." | "Rate your experience! Help build the community." |
| "This club has a Reliable badge based on their completed fixtures." | "This club has a 4.7 rating. Highly recommended!" |

---

## Prohibited patterns

- Exclamation marks in system messages (success confirmations, notifications, errors)
- Emoji in error states, trust displays, or any functional UI text
- "Looks like", "Seems like", "It appears that" — state the fact
- "Awesome", "Amazing", "Great", "Fantastic" as affirmations
- "Hey [name]" or "Hi there" as greeting patterns
- Passive voice for errors: say "That date is taken" not "The date could not be processed"
- Opaque error codes without plain English explanation

---

## Vocabulary

Canonical terms and prohibited substitutes are maintained in `shared/GLOSSARY.md`. All product copy must conform to that vocabulary. Specifically:

- Use **fixture**, not "match", "event", or "game" in system text
- Use **availability**, not "listing" or "post"
- Use **match request**, not "invite", "challenge", or "proposal"
- Use **find a game**, not "search matches" or "discover opponents"
- Use **trust badge**, not "rating", "stars", or "score"
- Use **accept / decline**, not "approve / reject"

Agent 3 (Naming & Verbal Identity) owns the full glossary. Questions about terminology go there.
