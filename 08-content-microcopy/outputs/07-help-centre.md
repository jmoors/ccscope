## HC-01 — How to find a game

**Title:** How to find a game

**Summary:** Finding available opponents for a given date.

---

Finding a game means searching for teams that have already posted their availability for a date you need.

**Step 1: Go to Find a game**

Select "Find a game" from the navigation. You will see a search form.

**Step 2: Enter your date and preferences**

Enter the date you need a game. You can filter by:
- Format (T20, 40-over, 50-over, timed declaration)
- Home or away
- Distance from your home ground
- Age group and gender

You do not need to fill in every field. A date is the only required field.

**Step 3: Review the results**

Results show teams with availability that matches your search. Each card shows the team name, format, home or away, ground name, and trust badge.

A format or age group notice may appear on a card if there is a difference between that team's preferences and yours. This is informational — you can still send a match request.

**Step 4: Send a match request**

Select a result to view the availability detail. If you want to arrange a fixture with that team, select "Send match request".

You can add a short optional note. The request expires after 7 days if not answered.

**If no results appear**

If no availability matches your search, post your own availability. Teams searching for a game on your date will then find you. The "Post availability" button on the empty results page carries over your date and format automatically.

## HC-02 — How to post availability

**Title:** How to post availability

**Summary:** Posting your team's availability so other teams can find you.

---

Posting availability means making your team visible to other teams that are searching for a game on a given date.

**When to post availability**

Post availability when you need a game and have not found one through search. Posting before searching is not recommended — search first in case a match already exists.

**Step 1: Go to Post availability**

Select "Post availability" from the navigation or the empty state on the search results page.

**Step 2: Fill in the details**

- **Date** — the date you are available
- **Format** — the format your team wants to play
- **Home or away** — whether you can host, travel, or either
- **Ground** — if you are available at home, select your ground
- **Available until** — the date after which your availability will be removed if no match is arranged

An optional note is available for any context you want to share with teams that find your listing.

**Step 3: Post**

Select "Post availability". Your availability will appear in search results immediately.

**Managing your availability**

Your posted availability appears in "My availability". From there you can remove it or update the details.

If you receive a match request against your availability, it will appear in "Requests". Accept or decline within 7 days.

## HC-03 — How trust badges work

**Title:** How trust badges work

**Summary:** What trust badges mean and how they are assigned.

---

Trust badges indicate a club's reliability based on their track record on the platform. They are shown on team profiles and search results to help fixture managers make informed decisions.

**The three badges**

| Badge | What it means |
|---|---|
| New | This club has completed fewer than a set number of fixtures on the platform. It does not mean anything negative — it simply means there is not yet enough history to assign a higher badge. All clubs start as New. |
| Reliable | This club has a positive track record of completing fixtures and receiving positive post-match reviews. |
| Established | This club has a long-standing positive track record over multiple seasons. |

**How badges are assigned**

Badges are based on:
- Whether confirmed fixtures were played as arranged
- Post-match reviews submitted by opposing teams after completed fixtures
- Patterns over time, not individual incidents

Short-notice cancellations (within 48 hours of the fixture) are weighted negatively. Weather cancellations do not affect the badge.

**What badges are not**

- They are not a star rating or percentage score
- They are not based on playing standard or ability
- Individual reviews are never shown publicly

**Reviews are private**

Every post-match review is private. The responses you submit are never shown to the reviewed club or any other user. Only aggregate signals feed into badge calculations.

**A note on new clubs**

All clubs begin with the "New" badge. New clubs are not penalised in search results purely for being new. The platform is designed to give new clubs a fair chance to build their track record.

## HC-04 — How match requests work

**Title:** How match requests work

**Summary:** Sending, receiving, accepting, and declining match requests.

---

A match request is how two teams agree to play a fixture. One team sends the request; the other accepts or declines.

**Sending a match request**

Find a team's availability through "Find a game". Open the availability detail and select "Send match request". You can add a short optional note. Once sent, the other team has 7 days to respond.

You will be notified as soon as they accept or decline.

**Receiving a match request**

Incoming match requests appear in "Requests" and on your dashboard under "Needs your attention". Open the request to see the details, including the team's trust badge and any note they have included.

You have 7 days to respond. You will receive a reminder 48 hours before the request expires.

**Accepting a request**

Select "Accept" to confirm the fixture. Both teams are notified immediately. The fixture appears in "My fixtures" for both teams.

**Declining a request**

Select "Decline" to decline. The requesting team is notified. You do not need to give a reason.

**What happens when a request expires**

If 7 days pass without a response, the request expires automatically. The requesting team is notified. No fixture is created.

**Withdrawing a request you sent**

You can withdraw a request from the request detail page, as long as it has not yet been accepted. Once accepted, a fixture is confirmed — use "Cancel fixture" on the fixture detail page to cancel a confirmed fixture.

## HC-05 — How to complete and review a fixture

**Title:** How to complete and review a fixture

**Summary:** Marking a fixture as played and leaving a post-match review.

---

**Completing a fixture**

After a fixture's date has passed, it is automatically marked as "Completed". You do not need to do anything.

**Leaving a review**

After a fixture is completed, you will receive a prompt to leave a review of the opposing team. Reviews take about one minute.

The review form has three questions, each with three options:
1. Did the fixture go ahead as arranged?
2. How would you describe the conduct of the opposing team?
3. Would you arrange a fixture with this team again?

There are no open text fields. There is no star rating. There is no public comment.

**Reviews are private**

Your review is private. It is never shown to the club being reviewed or to any other user. Reviews contribute to trust badge calculations over time using aggregate signals only.

**Review window**

The review window is open for 7 days after a fixture is completed. After 7 days, the window closes and the review can no longer be submitted.

You will receive one reminder 72 hours after the initial prompt if you have not yet submitted.

**Skipping a review**

You can skip a review. Skipping does not affect the other team's trust badge. If you skip three consecutive reviews, the platform will ask if you want to continue receiving review prompts.

## HC-06 — Managing your club profile

**Title:** Managing your club profile

**Summary:** For Club Administrators — editing club details, teams, grounds, and roster.

**Audience:** Club Administrators

---

The Club Administrator manages the club's profile on the platform. Fixture managers can view the club profile but cannot edit it.

**Club details**

To edit your club's name, home ground, and contact email, go to "Club" in the navigation, then select "Edit club profile".

Your club name and home ground are visible on your public club profile. Your contact email is not shown publicly.

**Teams**

A club can have multiple teams (1st XI, 2nd XI, Sunday XI, and so on). Each team can have its own fixture manager.

To add a team, go to "Club" > "Teams" > "Add team". Enter the team name and usual format. Fixture managers must be associated with a team before they can post availability or receive match requests.

To remove a team, the team must have no active fixtures. Cancel or complete all fixtures first.

**Grounds**

Your club's grounds are used when fixture managers post home availability. To add or edit grounds, go to "Club" > "Grounds".

**Roster**

The roster lists the umpires and scorers available through your club. Fixture managers can send support requests to people on the roster.

To add someone to the roster, go to "Club" > "Roster" > "Add person". Enter their name, role (umpire or scorer), and email address. They will receive support requests at that email address.

## HC-07 — Finding an umpire

**Title:** Finding an umpire

**Summary:** Sending an umpire request for a fixture.

---

Umpire requests go to umpires on your club's roster. You cannot currently request umpires from outside your club.

**Step 1: Go to the fixture**

Open the fixture you need an umpire for from "My fixtures".

**Step 2: Select "Find an umpire"**

This option appears in the "What's needed" panel when the fixture does not yet have a confirmed umpire.

**Step 3: Choose an umpire and send**

A list of available umpires from your club's roster is shown. Select one and add an optional note (format, start time, any other relevant detail).

Select "Send request". The umpire is notified immediately.

**If no umpires appear**

If no umpires appear, your club's roster may be empty. Ask your Club Administrator to add umpires to the roster.

**After the request is sent**

You will be notified when the umpire accepts or declines. If they decline, you can send a request to another umpire on the roster.

## HC-08 — Notification preferences

**Title:** Notification preferences

**Summary:** What notifications you receive and how to manage them.

---

The platform sends notifications when there is activity on your fixtures or requests.

**Mandatory notifications**

These cannot be turned off:
- Match requests sent to you
- Responses to match requests you have sent (accepted, declined, expired)
- Fixture status changes (confirmed, cancelled)

**Optional notifications**

You can turn these off in Settings > Notification preferences:
- Umpire and scorer request activity
- Post-match review prompts
- Weekly activity digest
- Deadline and expiry reminders

**Quiet hours**

By default, notifications are not delivered between 22:00 and 07:00. Notifications that arrive during quiet hours are held and delivered at 07:00. You can change your quiet hours in notification preferences.

**Email and in-app**

All notifications are sent by email and appear in your in-app notification inbox. Push notifications are not currently available.

## HC-09 — For umpires: how the platform works

**Title:** For umpires: how the platform works

**Audience:** Service providers (umpires, scorers)
**Summary:** How support requests work from an umpire's perspective.

---

Your role on the platform is as a service provider. You receive umpiring requests from fixture managers at clubs that have added you to their roster.

**Receiving a request**

When a fixture manager sends you a request, you receive an email and an in-app notification. The request includes:
- The fixture date and format
- The ground
- An optional note from the fixture manager

**Responding**

Open the request. Select "Accept" or "Decline".

If you accept, you are confirmed as umpire for that fixture and the fixture manager is notified. The fixture appears in your "Upcoming fixtures" list.

If you decline, the fixture manager is notified and can request another umpire.

**Requests expire**

If you do not respond, the request expires after the fixture manager's deadline. You will not be penalised for expired requests, but it is courteous to respond.

**Fixture reminders**

You will receive a reminder 3 days before any fixture you are confirmed on.

**Your account**

Your account is scoped to your umpiring role. You do not see the full fixture management interface. Your notification preferences are managed in Settings.

## HC-10 — Glossary of platform terms

**Title:** Platform terms explained

**Summary:** Key terms used on the platform and what they mean.

---

**Fixture**
A game record — whether or not all the details are confirmed. A fixture starts as partial (just a date) and fills in as the match is arranged. Not all fixtures reach "Confirmed" status.

**Availability**
A team's published intent to play on a given date. Availability is what appears in search results.

**Match request**
A team-to-team request to play a specific fixture. One team sends it; the other accepts or declines.

**Support request**
A request for an umpire or scorer for a fixture.

**Trust badge**
A badge that indicates a club's reliability. Badges are New, Reliable, or Established. See "How trust badges work" (HC-03).

**Awaiting opponent**
A fixture has a date but no confirmed opposition yet.

**Awaiting venue**
A fixture has an opponent but no confirmed ground.

**Awaiting officials**
A fixture needs a confirmed umpire or scorer.

**Awaiting response**
A match request has been sent but not yet answered.

**Confirmed**
All required parties are confirmed. The fixture is set to go ahead.

**Completed**
The fixture date has passed. It is marked completed automatically.

**Cancelled**
The fixture was called off before being played.

**Review**
A private 3-question form submitted after a completed fixture. Reviews are never shown publicly.

**Club Administrator**
The person responsible for managing a club's profile, teams, grounds, and roster on the platform.

**Fixture Manager**
The person responsible for finding games, posting availability, and managing fixtures for a specific team.

**Service provider**
An umpire, scorer, or coach. On this platform in v1, service providers are umpires contacted via your club's roster.
